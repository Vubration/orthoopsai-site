Fix Your Underperforming Listing With One AI Prompt

Published April 18, 2026 · 4 min read · Real Estate

You've got a property getting plenty of views on Zillow. Decent traffic. But the phone isn't ringing and showings aren't booking. Before you drop the price, check the copy.

Listing descriptions are the most underrated lever in real estate. Most agents write them in 10 minutes, slap "charming" and "move-in ready" in there, and call it done. Buyers click away. The listing sits.

The good news: fixing this takes about two minutes with AI.

The Prompt

Paste this into ChatGPT or Claude:

Copy this prompt → Here is a current property listing that isn't performing well: [paste your existing listing]. The property is [brief description]. Rewrite this listing to be more compelling — use active, specific language, lead with the strongest buyer benefit, eliminate vague phrases (like "must see!" or "won't last!"), and end with a soft call to action. Keep it under 200 words. Return only the rewritten listing copy.

What you'll get: a description that opens with what the buyer actually wants — a lifestyle, a feeling, a specific benefit — rather than a list of specs. The AI will strip out passive language and generic phrases and replace them with concrete, visual writing.

How to Get Better Results

Add a line telling the AI your target buyer: "this home is ideal for a family upgrading from a starter home" or "the likely buyer is a remote worker who wants a home office." Context produces better copy every time.

What to Watch For

AI sometimes invents features that aren't in the original. Always review the output and verify nothing was added. Your job is editor, not typist.
