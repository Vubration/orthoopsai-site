Why Generic ChatGPT Prompts Don't Work for Real Estate

Most agents try ChatGPT once, type something like "write a listing description for a 3-bedroom house," and get back something stiff and forgettable. They close the tab and never try again.

The problem isn't ChatGPT — it's the prompt. The AI is only as good as the instruction you give it. A well-written prompt includes:

  • Your role and audience — ChatGPT should know you're a real estate agent talking to a buyer or seller
  • The specific task — not "write an email" but "write a 7-day follow-up email to a seller lead who hasn't responded"
  • The tone you need — professional, warm, urgent, casual
  • Variables you fill in — property address, client name, price point, neighborhood

When you build prompts this way, you get output that sounds like you wrote it — not like a robot wrote it at 2 AM.

Prompt #1 — Single-Family Listing Description

This is the one you'll use most. Give it the key details and it builds a compelling description that highlights benefits, not just features.

Copy this prompt → You are a real estate copywriter helping a listing agent write property descriptions that sell. Write a compelling MLS listing description for the following property. Focus on lifestyle benefits, not just features. Use vivid but professional language. Keep it under 250 words. Do not use the words "stunning," "gorgeous," or "rare find." Property details: - Address: [ADDRESS] - Bedrooms: [#] | Bathrooms: [#] - Square footage: [SF] - Key features: [LIST 4-6 FEATURES — e.g., vaulted ceilings, updated kitchen, oversized lot, pool, 3-car garage] - Neighborhood highlights: [SCHOOLS, PROXIMITY TO HIGHWAYS/AMENITIES] - Price point: [$XXX,XXX] - Target buyer: [FIRST-TIME BUYER / MOVE-UP BUYER / INVESTOR / RETIREE]

Fill in the brackets and paste it into ChatGPT or Claude. You'll get a polished description in seconds. Want a different version? Add "give me two more variations — one more professional, one more emotional."

Prompt #2 — FSBO Cold Outreach Letter

FSBOs are one of the highest-converting lead sources for agents — if you approach them right. The key is acknowledging their choice first before making your case.

Copy this prompt → You are a real estate agent writing a prospecting letter to a homeowner selling their home For Sale By Owner (FSBO). The tone must be respectful, not pushy — acknowledge that they've chosen to sell themselves and genuinely offer to be a resource, not a salesperson. Briefly mention 2-3 specific ways a buyer's agent relationship benefits FSBO sellers (buyer's agents bringing qualified buyers, handling paperwork, etc.). Close with a soft call to action: a 15-minute no-obligation conversation. My name: [YOUR NAME] My brokerage: [BROKERAGE] Property address: [FSBO PROPERTY ADDRESS] My local market expertise: [NEIGHBORHOOD OR CITY YOU SPECIALIZE IN] Optional: recent sold comp nearby: [ADDRESS + PRICE if available]

Prompt #3 — 7-Day Lead Follow-Up Email

Most agents give up after 1-2 follow-ups. Studies consistently show it takes 6-8 touches before a lead responds. Here's a 7-day follow-up that doesn't sound desperate.

Copy this prompt → You are a real estate agent writing a follow-up email to a seller lead who filled out a home valuation form one week ago and hasn't responded to your first outreach. The email should be short (under 150 words), friendly, and curious — not pushy. Don't apologize for following up. Offer something of value (market update, comparable sales data) rather than just asking if they're still interested. End with a simple yes/no question to get a reply. My name: [YOUR NAME] Lead's name: [FIRST NAME if known, or "there"] Lead's address: [ADDRESS if known] Local market condition: [e.g., "seller's market, homes averaging 12 days on market"]

Prompt #4 — Responding to "Your Commission Is Too High"

Every agent faces this. The wrong response is defensive. The right response reframes value without being preachy. This prompt gives you a script you can actually say out loud.

Copy this prompt → You are a real estate agent who has just been told by a seller prospect "your commission is too high, I can get someone to do it for less." Write a professional, confident response that does NOT apologize for your commission, does NOT attack the competition, and DOES reframe the conversation around net proceeds and total value. Keep it conversational — this will be spoken out loud, not in an email. Under 200 words. My commission: [X%] One specific result I've achieved for recent clients: [e.g., "sold 3 homes above asking in the last 90 days in this neighborhood"] One differentiator: [e.g., "professional photography, staging consultation, targeted social media ads included"]

Prompt #5 — Buyer Consultation Needs Assessment

Walking into a buyer consultation without a structured discovery process is leaving money on the table. This prompt generates a complete set of questions that uncover motivation, urgency, and buying criteria.

Copy this prompt → You are a real estate agent preparing for a first-time buyer consultation. Generate a list of 12-15 discovery questions that uncover the buyer's motivation, timeline, financial readiness, must-haves vs. nice-to-haves, dealbreakers, and previous experience (if any) with the buying process. The questions should feel conversational, not like an interrogation. Group them into 3 sections: Lifestyle & Needs, Timeline & Finances, and Process & Expectations. Buyer type: [FIRST-TIME / MOVE-UP / INVESTOR / RELOCATION] Price range if known: [$XXX,XXX - $XXX,XXX] Location focus: [CITY/NEIGHBORHOOD]

How to Get the Most Out of These Prompts

A few practical tips that make a real difference:

Always read the output before sending. AI is fast, but it doesn't know your market or your voice as well as you do. Spend 30 seconds reading and adjusting. Add your name, a local reference, a specific detail. That's what turns AI output into something that sounds like you.

Ask for variations. If the first output isn't quite right, just say "give me three shorter versions" or "make this warmer/more professional." ChatGPT will iterate as many times as you need.

Save prompts that work. When you find a prompt that reliably gives you good results, save it in a Google Doc or notes app. Build your own library over time. Start with ours, then customize as you go.

Use it for the stuff you hate most. The listing descriptions, the FSBO letters, the awkward follow-ups — these are the things most agents avoid doing well. AI removes the friction. The agents who use these tools consistently will outproduce the ones who don't.
