5 ChatGPT Prompts for Lawyers You Can Use Today

These are real, deployable prompts. The brackets indicate placeholders you fill in before sending — no client-specific data ever goes into the AI.

Prompt 1: Client Intake Welcome Email

Write a professional new client welcome email for a [PRACTICE_AREA] law firm. The email should: - Address the client by first name using the placeholder [CLIENT_FIRST_NAME] - Welcome them warmly and confirm we received their inquiry - Explain the next steps: a [INTAKE_CALL_LENGTH]-minute consultation scheduled for [CONSULTATION_DATE_TIME] - List 3–4 things they should have ready for the call (documents, dates, key facts — keep generic, no specifics) - Include our firm name as [FIRM_NAME] and attorney name as [ATTORNEY_NAME] - Include office phone as [FIRM_PHONE] and email as [FIRM_EMAIL] - Close with a line that sets the tone: we're in their corner - Keep it under 200 words. Professional but approachable tone — not stuffy.

Prompt 2: Retainer Proposal Cover Letter

Write a professional retainer proposal cover letter for a [PRACTICE_AREA] attorney. The letter should: - Be addressed to [CLIENT_NAME] (placeholder) - Open with a brief summary of why the client came to us and what we're helping them accomplish — use [MATTER_DESCRIPTION] as a placeholder (e.g., "your [MATTER_TYPE] matter") - Explain the retainer fee structure: [RETAINER_AMOUNT] upfront, applied toward work at [HOURLY_RATE]/hour - Outline what's included: regular updates, direct attorney access, document review, court/negotiation representation where applicable - Include a clear call to action: sign and return the agreement by [DEADLINE_DATE] - Firm name: [FIRM_NAME] | Attorney: [ATTORNEY_NAME] | Phone: [FIRM_PHONE] - Tone: confident, professional, reassuring — the client should feel they're making the right decision - Length: 250–300 words

Prompt 3: Google Review Response (Ethics-Safe)

Write a professional response to a [POSITIVE/NEGATIVE/NEUTRAL] Google review for a [PRACTICE_AREA] law firm. The response must: - Be completely ethics-safe: do NOT mention the reviewer's name, their case type, any outcome, any legal strategy, or any detail that could identify the matter or confirm attorney-client relationship - Thank the reviewer genuinely without confirming or denying any specifics - For negative reviews: acknowledge the experience empathetically, do not be defensive, invite them to call [FIRM_PHONE] to resolve directly - For positive reviews: thank them warmly and mention we're here if they ever need us again - Keep it under 75 words - Tone: warm, professional, human — not corporate boilerplate

Prompt 4: Practice Area Blog Post (Demand-Driven SEO)

Write a 700-word blog post for a [PRACTICE_AREA] law firm. The topic is: "[SPECIFIC_LEGAL_QUESTION_YOUR_CLIENTS_ASK]" (example: "What Happens if I Miss a Child Support Payment in Florida?") The blog post should: - Open with the specific question or fear the reader has — speak directly to their situation - Give a clear, accessible answer in plain English (no excessive legal jargon) - Include 2–3 key points or subheadings that break down the answer - Note that every situation is different and readers should consult an attorney for their specific case - End with a soft call to action: "[FIRM_NAME] handles [PRACTICE_AREA] cases throughout [STATE/REGION]. Call [FIRM_PHONE] for a free [X]-minute consultation." - Tone: authoritative but approachable. Like a knowledgeable friend explaining the law, not a textbook. - Include a natural mention of the firm's city/region for local SEO.

Prompt 5: Consultation No-Show Follow-Up Sequence

Write a 2-email follow-up sequence for a law firm to re-engage a prospective client who missed or cancelled their consultation without rescheduling. Email 1 (send 24 hours after the missed appointment): - Address them by first name: [PROSPECT_FIRST_NAME] - Keep it brief, low-pressure, no guilt - Acknowledge things come up, offer to reschedule - Include scheduling link: [SCHEDULING_LINK] - Sign off from [ATTORNEY_NAME] at [FIRM_NAME] - 60–80 words max Email 2 (send 5 days after Email 1 if no response): - Final check-in tone — still warm, no pressure - Mention we hold spots for people who reached out, but availability is limited - If they want to talk, they can still reach us at [FIRM_PHONE] or [FIRM_EMAIL] - If the timing isn't right, wish them well genuinely — leave the door open - 50–70 words max
