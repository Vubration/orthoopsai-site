What AI Can't Do for Real Estate Agents

Before you go all-in, be clear on where the limits are. AI can't:

  • Know your local market the way you do. It needs you to feed it the relevant context — the micro-neighborhood dynamics, the HOA reputation, the fact that the builder-grade cabinets in that subdivision are always a negotiating point. That knowledge comes from you. AI just helps you articulate it faster.
  • Replace the relationship. Clients still want to talk to a person. AI makes you faster and more prepared — it doesn't make the call for you.
  • Access your MLS. ChatGPT doesn't know your market data unless you paste it in. Combine it with Perplexity for live web data and your own MLS exports for real comps.
  • Guarantee accuracy. Always review AI-generated market statistics, neighborhood descriptions, and anything that could mislead a client. You're the licensed professional — the AI is the assistant.

The Fastest Way to Get Started

If you're new to AI tools, the fastest path is:

  1. Start with ChatGPT free. Go to chat.openai.com. Don't pay until you're using it every day.
  2. Use proven prompts, not your own. The biggest mistake new users make is spending time crafting prompts from scratch. Start with templates built specifically for real estate tasks so you get good output on your first try.
  3. Pick one use case and nail it. Listing descriptions are the easiest starting point. Get good at that, then add expired outreach, then add market emails. Layer, don't flood.
  4. Add Perplexity once you're comfortable. Use it before listing presentations and buyer consults to research the neighborhood quickly.
  5. Add Otter if you do a lot of consultations. It changes how you show up in those meetings — fully present instead of half-focused on notes.

The agents who fail with AI try to implement everything at once, get overwhelmed, and go back to their old workflow. The agents who win pick one tool, build a habit, and expand from there.
